# Neighborhood Context: Brookline Village

## Overview

Brookline Village is the historic center of the Town of Brookline, MA. It serves as the municipal core — home to Town Hall, the main public library branch, and the town's original commercial district along Washington, Boylston, and Harvard Streets.

## The Lindens: Historical Context

19 Linden Street sits within "The Lindens" — Brookline's first planned residential development, conceived in 1843 by Thomas Aspinwall Davis as a "garden suburb." The original development featured Greek Revival and Gothic Revival homes on spacious lots for Boston's merchant class.

In the late 1800s and early 1900s, population growth led to the subdivision of many large estates and replacement of several original homes with multifamily apartment buildings and triple-deckers. **19 Linden St is described as the "best-preserved survivor"** of the original 1843 subdivision.

- Chronic undersupply of quality housing near Longwood

### Brookline Public Schools
- Consistently ranked among the best in Massachusetts
- Significant driver of family demand
- Supports long-term property value appreciation

### Boston University / Boston College Proximity
- Faculty and staff housing demand
- Graduate/professional student family housing
