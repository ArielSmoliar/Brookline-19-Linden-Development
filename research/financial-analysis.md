# Financial Analysis: 19 Linden St Development

## Land Economics

| Metric | Value |
|---|---|
| Asking price | $5,950,000 |
| Lot size | 17,698 sq ft |
| Price per sq ft (lot) | $336/sf |
| Price per buildable sf (FAR 1.0) | $336/sf |
| Price per as-of-right unit (~15) | ~$397,000/unit |

**Context:** For a multifamily development to achieve 20%+ returns, land should typically represent 15-25% of total project revenue. At $5.95M land and ~$16.5M revenue (as-of-right), land = 36% of revenue. This is too high.

## Key Financial Conclusions

1. **As-of-right at $5.95M is a loss** in every realistic scenario
2. **As-of-right at $4-4.5M is marginal** — requires $1,200+/sf sale prices for acceptable margins
3. **Adaptive reuse improves margins** through Historic Tax Credits, premium pricing, and shorter delay
4. **40B density helps but doesn't solve** the problem at $5.95M due to affordable unit drag and long carry
5. **The deal works at $4-4.5M land** with either as-of-right luxury product or 40B density at $1,200/sf sales
6. **Target acquisition: $4.0-4.5M** for a risk-adjusted 15-20% development margin
