# Zoning & Entitlements: 19 Linden St, Brookline Village

## Zoning District: M-1.0

The property sits in Brookline's **M-1.0 (Multi-family Residence)** zoning district.

- **"M"** = Multi-family Residence — permits single-family, two-family, and multi-family (4+ units)
- **"1.0"** = Maximum Floor Area Ratio

**Note:** The exact setback, open space, and parking figures must be confirmed from **Table 5.01** of the Brookline Zoning By-Law. The table is available as a scanned PDF from the Town Clerk's office.

### Building Envelope Estimate

Assuming 40 ft height (4 stories):
- 17,698 GFA / 4 stories = ~4,425 sq ft footprint (~25% lot coverage)
- After setbacks, effective buildable footprint estimated at 8,850-10,600 sq ft
- 4,425 sq ft footprint fits within this envelope

Assuming 35 ft height (3 stories):
- 17,698 GFA / 3 stories = ~5,900 sq ft footprint (~33% lot coverage)
- Tighter but likely workable

### Binding Constraint

The **density formula** (lot area per unit) is the binding constraint at 15-16 units. The FAR of 1.0 does not further limit unit count given typical unit sizes.

## Entitlement Pathways

### Path 1: As-of-Right Development
- **Units:** 14-16
- **Timeline:** 6-12 months (plus 18-month demo delay if demolishing)
- **Risk:** Low entitlement risk; high financial risk at current asking price
- **Process:** Standard site plan review, building permit

### Path 2: Special Permit (Section 5.43)
- **What it does:** Board of Appeals can waive yard/setback requirements if counterbalancing amenities are provided
- **Units:** 14-16 (density formula unchanged)
- **Benefit:** Larger building footprint, more efficient site plan
- **Timeline:** 9-18 months
- **Risk:** Moderate — requires Board approval

### Path 3: Chapter 40B Comprehensive Permit
- **What it does:** Overrides local zoning for affordable housing projects
- **Units:** 25-30+ (potentially double as-of-right)
- **Requirements:** 25% of units must be affordable (BMI < 80% AMI)
- **Timeline:** 18-30 months for permitting alone
- **Risk:** High — Brookline is politically hostile to 40B; check safe harbor status
- **Key question:** Does Brookline currently have safe harbor? If yes, the ZBA can deny the comprehensive permit.

### Path 4: MBTA Communities Act Overlay
- **What it does:** State-mandated overlay districts allowing multifamily housing near transit
- **Potential benefit:** May allow higher density than M-1.0 base zoning
- **Status:** Brookline adopted compliance zoning Fall 2023
- **Key question:** Is 19 Linden St within the Section 3A overlay boundary?
- **If yes:** Could unlock additional as-of-right density without 40B process
