# Development Strategies: 19 Linden St

## Strategy 1: Adaptive Reuse + New Construction (Recommended)

### Concept
Preserve and restore the 1843 Greek Revival house as 2-3 signature residences. Build a new 10-12 unit multifamily building behind the existing structure, taking advantage of the deep lot.

### Advantages
- **Avoids 18-month demolition delay** — preservation, not demolition
- **Federal Historic Tax Credit** — 20% of qualified rehabilitation costs
- **Massachusetts Historic Tax Credit** — additional state incentive
- **Reduces community opposition** — preservation aligns with neighborhood values
- **Marketing differentiation** — "live in a landmark" commands premium pricing
- **Faster to market** — no demo delay, simpler permitting

### Challenges
- Historic rehabilitation costs are 15-25% higher than new construction
- Must meet Secretary of Interior Standards for tax credit eligibility
- Existing structure may constrain unit layouts
- Split construction approach (rehab + new) adds complexity

### Pro Forma Summary
- **Acquisition:** $4.5M (negotiated)
- **Total dev cost:** ~$17.6M
- **Revenue:** ~$19.2M (including tax credits)
- **Margin:** ~9.3%

## Strategy 2: Full Demolition + New Construction (As-of-Right)

### Concept
Demolish existing structure after 18-month delay. Build a 14-16 unit multifamily building maximizing the FAR 1.0 envelope.

### Advantages
- **Maximum design flexibility** — no constraints from existing structure
- **Lower construction cost per sf** — all new construction, no rehab complexity
- **Efficient floor plans** — purpose-built for market

### Challenges
- **18-month demolition delay** — certain, with ~$540-712K carrying cost
- **Intense community opposition** — demolishing the "best-preserved survivor"
- **No Historic Tax Credits** — lose 20%+ in incentives
- **Risk of emergency LHD designation** — community could petition for Local Historic District during delay period
- **Political fallout** — Brookline Town Meeting and media attention

### Pro Forma Summary
- **Acquisition:** $4.5M (negotiated)
- **Total dev cost:** ~$15.8M
- **Revenue:** ~$16.5-18.0M
- **Margin:** 4-14% (highly sensitive to sale price)

---

## Strategy 3: Chapter 40B Comprehensive Permit

### Concept
Apply for a comprehensive permit under MA Chapter 40B to override local zoning and build 25-30 units with 25% affordable.

### Advantages
- **Doubles unit count** — dramatically improves per-unit land cost
- **Overrides local zoning** — height, density, setback, parking relief
- **Addresses housing need** — aligns with state policy goals
- **Potential political allies** — affordable housing advocates

### Challenges
- **Brookline is hostile to 40B** — organized opposition, experienced legal resistance
- **Safe harbor status** — if Brookline has safe harbor, ZBA can deny the application
- **25% affordable requirement** — reduces blended revenue per unit
- **2+ year entitlement timeline** — carrying costs compound
- **Legal costs** — potential Housing Appeals Committee proceedings
- **Combining 40B with historic demolition** — dual political opposition

### Critical First Step
**Verify Brookline's Chapter 40B safe harbor status immediately.** If active, this strategy is blocked.

### Pro Forma Summary
- **Acquisition:** $4.5M (negotiated)
- **Total dev cost:** ~$23.9M
- **Revenue:** ~$22.9-24.7M
- **Margin:** -4% to +3% (marginal even with density)

---

## Strategy 4: Land Bank / Hold for Rezoning

### Concept
Acquire at a discount. Hold the property while MBTA Communities Act forces upzoning of transit-adjacent parcels. Sell or develop when zoning improves.

### Advantages
- **Regulatory tailwinds are real** — Brookline must zone for 6,990 units
- **Low execution risk** — no construction, no entitlement battles
- **Optionality** — can develop or sell when timing is right
- **Income potential** — rent existing house during hold period

### Challenges
- **Carrying cost** — $360-475K/year in financing costs
- **Timeline uncertainty** — upzoning could take 2-5+ years
- **No guarantee parcel is included** — overlay may not cover this specific lot
- **Capital tied up** — opportunity cost of funds

### Financial Logic
- Acquire at $4-4.5M
- Hold costs: ~$400K/year
- If zoning changes to FAR 2.0+, lot value could reach $7-9M
- 3-year hold: $4.5M + $1.2M carry = $5.7M basis → sell at $8M = ~$2.3M profit (40%)

## Recommended Approach

**Lead with Strategy 1 (Adaptive Reuse)** while pursuing due diligence on all paths:

1. **Negotiate acquisition to $4-4.5M** — anchor on as-of-right economics
2. **Engage preservation architect** — develop adaptive reuse concept for the Greek Revival house
3. **Verify 40B safe harbor and MBTA overlay** — keep density upside options open
4. **Consult Preservation Commission early** — build goodwill, understand their priorities
5. **Design new construction to complement** the historic house — deep lot allows significant new building behind existing structure
6. **Apply for Historic Tax Credits** — 20%+ cost offset on rehabilitation work
7. **Pre-sell or gauge market** — test $1,200-1,400/sf pricing for units with historic character

**Fallback:** If adaptive reuse is not financially viable after detailed design, pivot to full demolition with negotiated land price and pre-committed buyers.
